Forecasting Overdispersed Arrivals via Bayesian Simulation

Xiaowei Zhang

Abstract

A crucial input for call center staffing and scheduling is an accurate forecast of future call volumes. We propose a new dynamic Poisson model for the call arrival process and apply a Bayesian approach to predict the distributions of the unobservable arrival rates. Both interday forecasting and intraday updating are considered. Different Monte Carlo methods are developed in light of their respective offline and online nature. Applied to two real data sets, our proposed methods exhibit significant competitiveness in comparison with sophisticated benchmarks that are based on the popular “root-unroot” data transformation. Our study suggests that, if a Poisson assumption is reasonable for call arrivals, to model the arrival data directly is statistically more efficient than the transformed data and produces more accurate forecasts.
