HKU Business School

Biography

I am an assistant professor of business analytics in HKU Business School. My research interests include stochastic simulation, decision analytics, and machine learning.

As an academic, my goal is to identify and study key structures in stochastic systems to deliver better decisions, increasing efficiency and reducing risk of the operations. The growing data availability and computing power provide vast opportunities to design new statistical tools to facilitate the decision-making process. In this regard, I aim to develop general methodologies in statistics, stochastics, and simulation for decision analytics and integrate these tools to solve real-world challenges.
