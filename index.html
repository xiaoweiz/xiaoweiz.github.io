About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial Engineering and Decision Analytics at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. As a researcher, my goal is to identify and study key structures in stochastic systems to deliver better decisions, increasing efficiency and reducing risk of the operations.

The growing data availability and computing power provide vast opportunities to design new statistical tools to facilitate the decision-making process. In this regard, I aim to develop general methodologies in statistics, stochastics, and simulation for decision analytics and integrate these tools to solve real-world challenges.

Affine Point Processes: Approximation and Efficient Simulation

We establish a central limit theorem and a large deviations principle for affine point processes, which are stochastic models of correlated event timing widely used in finance and economics. These limit results generate closed-form approximations to the distribution of an affine point process. They also facilitate the construction of an asymptotically optimal importance sampling estimator of tail probabilities. Numerical tests illustrate our results.
Xiaowei Zhang, Jose Blanchet, Kay Giesecke, Peter W. Glynn
Mathematics of Operations Research,2015
